👥 Characters

The Characters section is where you build your personal database of people. Each character profile stores important information such as how you met them, first impressions, personality notes, and trust metrics. This allows you to organize and understand the individuals who appear in your life story.

📖 Journal

The Journal is where memories are recorded. Every meaningful interaction, event, or story can be written here along with the characters involved. These entries become part of your growing archive and also contribute to your daily streak. Over time, the journal becomes a detailed narrative of experiences.

🕵️ Intel

The Intel section stores observations, rumors, information, or insights you collect about people and situations. Think of it as your private intelligence archive, a place where you store things you notice, learn, or hear. Intel entries often connect back to characters and events.

🕰 Timeline

The Timeline brings together events from your Journal, Intel files, and Logs into one chronological story. It allows you to see how moments unfold over time and how different characters intersect within your experiences. Each event is represented with a colored marker to quickly identify the type of information being displayed.

  • Blue — Interaction Logs
    Direct interactions or moments recorded inside a character’s profile.
  • Red — Intel Files
    Observations, gossip, or information collected about people or situations.
  • Yellow — Journal Entries
    Memories and events you personally record in the journal.

By combining these sources, the timeline transforms your archive into a living narrative of events — showing how relationships evolve and how moments connect across time.

🗄 Database

The Database page provides a complete table view of every character stored in PeopleBook. Here you can search, manage, and navigate through profiles efficiently. If the Characters page focuses on profiles, the Database page focuses on overview and management.
